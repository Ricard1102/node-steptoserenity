About Us

Welcome to the Beute Clinic, here you will find the podiatry service you were looking for

We are waiting for you :)

The clinic has recently been refurbished with the patient’s needs and comfort at the centre of the renovation. There is parking directly in front of the clinic, and the doorways have been widened to accommodate wheel chair access. We have tea and coffee making facilities and there are male, female and disabled toilets on site. The clinical room has been fitted with the latest state of the art equipment to comply with national regulations in health and safety. The treatment room has also been fitted with a monitor to enable patients to watch treatment as it is taking place.

Today, the treatment of medical instruments and accessories is an indispensable part of quality assurance in medically-oriented practices. We aim to maintain the highest level of hygiene standards and have invested in the new Thermoclean which cleans all the equipment by removing protein deposits and debris using an enzymatic tablet. The instruments are then placed in a sealed pouch and placed in the new Euronda Autoclave which sterilises for 30 minutes at 134 degrees. Floors are hoovered, and where applicable steam cleaned; the treatment couch is re-dressed in disposable covers after every appointment and all surface areas are cleaned with disposable disinfection wipes.

Description on other treatments

Diabetic Foot Assessment

A 20 minute foot assessment using ultrasound to measure the amount of blood flow to the feet; together with a test to check for any loss of protective sensation. A full report of the assessment is provided.

Nail surgery

A one hour appointment to remove the whole or part of the effected toe nail. A chemical called phenol is used to ensure the offending portion of nail does not grow back. Nail surgery without phenalisation is also provided. Patients are assessed for suitability of surgery and referred to the hospital if deemed unsuitable.

Verruca Treatment

In this 30 minute session, liquid nitrogen and salicylic acid is used to freeze/thaw and destroy the skin cells affected by the verruca virus. After treatment, a sore blister will form, followed by a scab, which will detach 7-10 days later. This usually takes 15- 20 minutes. Treatments are usually spaced a week apart.

Biomechanical Assessment

Examines the manor in which feet function when weight bearing and non weight bearing as well as during gait. This test checks for abnormalities and possible causes of pain in the foot, ankle, knees and lower back. Insoles or orthotics may be recommended.

Orthotics

A liner for your footwear to correct walking and reduce common biomechanical problems such as heel pain, to relieve stress from injured areas and provide support to overloaded joints. A wide variety of customised orthotics and pre-fabricated orthotics are available.

Our Team

Our staff are all fully qualified podiatrists with degrees in podiatric medicine and registered with the Health and Care Professions Council, and Society of Chiropodists and Podiatrists. They have worked through out the UK in the NHS and private care. They have specialist expertise  in the care of diabetic feet and wounds. Members of the team have worked on hospital wards, A&E, Ambulatory care, in Orthopedic clinics, the community, and in a domiciliary setting. They are able to treat a wide range of foot problems including fungating wounds, necrobiosis lipodica diabactorum, acute and chronic osteomyelitis, infected gangrenous limbs, wounds caused through neglect and post surgical wounds.
